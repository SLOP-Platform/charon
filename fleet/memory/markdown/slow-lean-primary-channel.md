Operator (2026-07-12): "slow down, you are going too fast on the text I see; subsessions no need to slow down."

**Why:** the operator reads the primary channel in real time and gets overwhelmed by volume/pace of visible text. Background sub-session work is not read live, so it has no such limit.

**How to apply:** in the PRIMARY session, minimize on-screen text — short answers, one decision at a time, lead with the table/answer, cut preamble and recap. Slow the cadence (fewer moves per turn; wait). Push depth/verbosity into sub-sessions, which can run full-speed and detailed. This sharpens [[answer-concisely-and-say-where-to-run]], [[pause-after-question-or-action]], and [[work-in-phases-gather-then-wait]] — the constraint is specifically the VOLUME + SPEED of operator-visible text, not the amount of underlying work.
